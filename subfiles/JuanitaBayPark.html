Juanita Bay Park
Juanita Bay Park is a combination of various forms of wetland that run along the portion of 98th ave NE just beyond Juanita Village.

A popular attraction of the park is the bridge which crosses the wetlands and ends just outside the city.

The wetlands are comprised of six seperate plant communities:

The Wet Meadow: Wet meadows are perpetually saturated to an extent that prevents the growth of trees.

The Marsh: Marshes are similar to wet meadows but exceed the threshold of water saturation that the soil can handle resulting in a waterlogged area.

The Wooded Wetland: A portion of the wetland with low enough water saturation to allow trees and various shrubbery to grow.

The intermittent Marsh: Portions of the marsh that are not always waterlogged.
